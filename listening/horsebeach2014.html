Horsebeach's debut album Horsebeach (2014): review

April 17th, 2021

The album is 10 tracks long

I am not a fan of Horsebeach nor do I listen to their music, but this album has stuck with me and has been replayed many times. 6 of the 10 tracks I really enjoyed, while the other songs were okay or not memorable enough for me to go back and listen to (I marked my favorites with a #). I'll elaborate on why those stood out after going through the first songs; I want to review the whole album.

Faded Eyes

We fade in to a distorted guitar strumming rhythmically alongside a cymbal; transitioning into continued guitar with distorted drums being played slowly. This song is good, but doesn't change enough, it maintains the same tempo the entire song. The vocals try to raise the songs mood but don't do enough. "You hide the tears that fall from your faded eyes", lyrics don't stand out in anyway. Fade out.

There is no transition between tracks.

Yesterdays

Abrupt explosion of several different instruments playing a somber melody. Nothing stands outs.

Even

The vocals in this song became annoying to me, I usually skip it; specifically the chorus when Ryan Kennedy says "we are falling further down".

A Place Like This

Okay, now I start to actually enjoy the album a lot. For me to really like a song, I have to feel some emotional reactoin when I listen to it, wether negative or positive. Usually it goes, I feel sad when listening to emotional alternative music, and "good" when I listen to loud trap music. I figure, if this artist was able to get me to feel this emotion with their music, maybe the song is objectibely good in soem way. I don't know how true this is but I start to dissect the vocals, instruments, lyrics, and subconcious biases I might have of the song. "A Place Like This" makes me sad specifically through the vocals and tone; similar to the opening track we are eventually introduced to a somber horsebeach melody.

But when Kennedy first says, "So why does she pay for their mistakes" it triggers a guitar...
